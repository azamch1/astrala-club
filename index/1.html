A website that is committed to improving society through helping the children of the world.

   

Astrala is a legendary figure, a cosmic child. The myth of Astrala was created by English-American author Dr. Richard S. Kirby (born 1949) around 1986.

    Astrala's story is a story of the Children of Tomorrow. Astrala is a girl aged perhaps seven. She is a girl of mixed race, of uncertain background, a child of the earth and of the stars. ['Astrala' is a word which comes from the ancient Greek. It means, approximately, 'Daughter of the Stars'].

    The myth of Astrala begins with her 'Birth-Quake' when she mysteriously appears on earth with a message about the world's orphans. Since then she and her cosmic cat, Cosma, and a friendly robot, travel the earth and outer Space with a message of hope and love for the world's children everywhere.

    Author Richard Kirby is developing the Astrala story in a series of tales. An Astrala musical is also expected. To inquire about adapting the Astrala story for local or multi-media use, please contact Dr. Kirby at the World Network of Religious Futurists.

    In addition to the Astrala Clinic in Pakistan, there is an Astrala community in Orissa, India, and an Astrala trail in Oklahoma, USA. If you would like to create an Astrala club, trail or community, please contact Dr. Kirby at the World Network of Religious Futurists.

The drawing of Astrala below our banner was done by Oklahoman Marianne Sansing in 1995.
